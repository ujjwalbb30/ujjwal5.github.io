PROJECTS

All - Terrain Robot - Minor Project

I fabricated an all-terrain robot consisting of a suspension-induced chassis and arduino mega 800 with the help of my 3 team-members. DC motors were used to drive the tyres whereas Johnson motor and Servo motors were used to actuate the pick-n-place gripper. Whole robot was energised with the help of a LiPo 12V 2200mAh battery. The CAD assembly was created with the help of CATIA V5.

Portable Mechanical Charger - Major Project

I designed and fabricated a portable human powered mechanical charger along with my 3 team members. We Used 3 pairs of 69 teeth and 23 teeth Spur gears to produce a compound gear train with the final ratio of 1:27 to give motion to dynamo. When applied sufficient force, we were able to generate 12V output for charging with the help of a diode bridge rectifier.

HAVELLS INDIA LTD.

Havells India Ltd. is one of the largest Fast Moving Electrical Goods (FMEG) company in India. I worked for the R&D of pumps at Center for Research and Innovation (CRI) of Havells. I engaged in all phases of design process for multitude of pump products, components, parts, assemblies, and subassemblies, including drafting, dimensioning, tolerancing, prototyping and documenting results.

Water-Ingress Protection for MonoBlock Pumps

One of the most important project I worked on. The objective of the project was to find the cheapest solution that can be implemented in all monoblock pumps design in order to counter the water-ingress problem in these pumps. New designs were developed to contain a special type of seal and the solution was implemented in 14 pump SKUs. A patent application was also filed which is still being processed.

CAD layout of Pumps R&D Lab

I also worked for the finalisation of Purchase Order Request for the pumps testing and validation lab at CRI. After getting the final layout of area by the administration, I prepared the CAD models of all the components to be placed in the lab and developed a final CAD layout of the lab. It included design of testing tanks, tools table, fixtures for equipments, pipeline layout etc.

Cranfield University

Advanced Vehicle Engineering Centre (AVEC)

Time spent at Cranfield University became a defining moment in my life. I worked as a visiting researcher at Advanced Vehicle Engineering Centre (AVEC), School of Aerospace, Transport and Manufacture (SATM), Cranfield University. I was selected by Dr. Stefano Longo so that I can work with Mr. Manlio Valerio Morganti on the development of a Hardware-in-Loop (HIL) test rig for Electrical Vehicle Thermal Management System. I joined the University on 28th February 2017, and completed my internship on 14th July 2017.

The project was funded by Arrival (formerly, Charge Automotive Ltd.) which manufactures electric vehicles and is looking forward to deliver an optimisation methodology for the Thermal Management System for the same.

After completing the literature review and understanding the gaps in research, I worked on comparsion of heat exchangers, heat exchanger fluids and the Data Acquisition (DAQ) systems. I submitted a brief report on these comparisons as well. I worked on developing CAD models of the test rig, simulation chamber and their components on CATIA and rendered them on Keyshot for presentation purposes (as shown above). A virtual design helped us in finalising the Purchase ORder Request (POR).

Managing work space environment, working with researchers from different backgrounds, achieving time bound targets, complying with environment, health and safety rules of the research centre, ethics of work and communication, understanding the research methodology, were some new things I learned at Cranfield and I forever cherish those valuable learnings.
